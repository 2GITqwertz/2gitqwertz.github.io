Box Breathing.

HBox breathing follows a clear and structured rhythm with four equal phases. You inhale, hold, exhale, and hold again — each for the same amount of time. This creates a steady and balanced breathing pattern.

Because of its simple structure, box breathing is often described as easy to follow and predictable, making it suitable for moments when a clear rhythm feels helpful.

Box Breathing

Box breathing follows a clear and structured rhythm with four equal phases.

Classic Pattern (4-4-4-4)

Inhale 4 seconds - Hold 4 seconds - Exhale 4 seconds - Hold 4 seconds.

Extended Exhale Pattern (4-4-6-2)

Inhale 4 seconds - Hold 4 seconds - Exhale 6 seconds - Hold 2 seconds.

When to Practice

Useful before focused work, during mentally demanding tasks, or whenever you want to slow a racing mind.

How to Practice

  • Follow the rhythm gently without forcing.
  • Keep pauses relaxed and unstrained.
  • Prioritize calm flow over perfect precision.
